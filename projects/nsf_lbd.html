Literature-based discovery for NSF grand funding

1. Background

National Science Foundation (aka NSF) is the U.S Federal agency sponsoring for academic research grants. In recent years, seeking funding becomes increasingly competitive and challenging (funding rate is 26% in 2021). For this reason, the capacity to predict the topics of future grants is practically useful for prospective writers.

2. Related works and research objectives

Many studies have been carried out to examine the contributing factors to the success of grant applications. There have been two general approaches:

  • Conventional approaches such as applying writing styles and linguistics features into grant proposals. These characteristics are necessary for writing but gradually not the most influencers on funding success.
  • There is very little work has been involved to model the past trending and performance of research topics from historical successful grants, but still no clear recommendations or rationale for the continuation of these topics in the future.
Therefore, the main objective of this research is to provide recommendation for topic prediction from historical grants that could help future writers have the ideas in developing their own proposal.
